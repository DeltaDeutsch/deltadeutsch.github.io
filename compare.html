Northern and Southern Hemisphere Comparisons Page

Plotting Weather Data

On all of the scatterplots, there are fewer cities appearing in the Southern Hemisphere. That does not indicate smaller correlation. It indicates a limited number of cities in the Southern Hemisphere. The Southern Hemisphere has far fewer cities than the Northern Hemisphere for two primary reasons: more Ocean (less land) and fewer people than in the Northern Hemisphere. The Southern Hemisphere, the half of Earth south of the Equator, contains 81% water (20% more than the Northern Hemisphere) from four oceans, including the Indian, South Atlantic, Southern, and South Pacific). The population of the Southern Hemisphere is 800 million, only 10-12% of the total world population of 7.3 billion. The continent of Antarctica contains the geographic South Pole and is entirely in the Southern Hemisphere. Because the Southern Hemisphere has significantly more ocean and less land, climates tend to be milder than similar latitudes in the Northern Hemisphere. This is because water heats up and cools more slowly than land.
